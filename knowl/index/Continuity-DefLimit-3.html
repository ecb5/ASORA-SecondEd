To be sure, limits were always lurking in the background. In his attempts to justify his calculations, Newton used what he called his doctrine of Ultimate Ratios. For example, the ratio \(\frac{(x+h)^2-x^2}{h} = \frac{2xh+h^2}{h} = 2x+h\) becomes \(2x\) ultimately, or at the last instant of time before \(h\) becomes zero. Newton would have called \(h\) an “evanescent quantity” or “vanishing quantity” ([4], p. 33).
