Problem 3.4.1.

Without using Taylor’s Theorem, represent the following functions as power series expanded about 0 (i.e., in the form \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_nx^n\)).

(a)

Use the result of Problem 3.2.15 to find the series representation of
\begin{equation*} \ln(1+x^2) \text{.} \end{equation*}

(b)

\(f(x)=\ln\left(2+x\right)\)
Hint.
\(2+x=2\left(1+\frac{x}{2}\right)\)

(c)

Use the Geometric Series to find the series representation of
\begin{equation*} f(x) = \frac{1}{1+x^2} \end{equation*}

(d)

Use the result of part (c) to find the series representation of
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\frac{x}{1+x^2} \text{.} \end{equation*}

(e)

Use the result of part (d) to find the series representation of
\begin{equation*} f(x)= \frac{1}{1-x^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(f)

Use the result of part (c) to find the series representation of
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle f(x) =\arctan \left(x^3\right) \end{equation*}

(g)

\(f(x)=a^x\text{,}\) where \(a\gt 0\)
Hint.
\(a^x=e^{\ln\,\left(a^x\right)}\)
