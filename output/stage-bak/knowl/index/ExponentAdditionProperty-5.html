Returning to our definition of a power series, consider the series
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty x^n=1+x+x^2+\cdots \text{.} \end{equation*}
If we multiply this series by \((1-x)\text{,}\) we obtain
\begin{align*} (1-x)(1+x+x^2+\cdots)=(1\amp{}+x+x^2+\cdots)\\ \amp{}-(x+x^2+x^3+\cdots)\\ =1\amp{}. \end{align*}
