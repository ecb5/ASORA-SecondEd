Definition 6.1.10. Convergence of a Sequence.

Let \(\left(s_n\right)=\left(s_1,s_2,s_3,\ldots\right)\) be a sequence of real numbers and let \(s\) be a real number. We say that \(\left(\boldsymbol{s}_{\boldsymbol{n}}\right)\) converges to \(\boldsymbol{s}\) and write \(\limitt{n}{\infty}{s_n}=s\) provided for any \(\eps>0\text{,}\) there is a real number \(N\) such that if \(n>N\text{,}\) then \(|s_n-s|\lt \eps\text{.}\)
