Problem 3.2.13.

(a)

Use the Geometric series to obtain a series for
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\frac{1}{1+x}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(b)

Use the result in part (a) to obtain a series for
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\frac{1}{1+x^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(c)

Use the result in part (b) to obtain the series
\begin{align*} \arctan x\amp{}=x-\frac{1}{3}x^3+\frac{1}{5}x^5-\cdots\\ \amp{}=\sum_{n=0}^\infty(-1)^n \frac{1}{2n+1}x^{2n+1}. \end{align*}

(d)

Use the result of part (c) to conclude that
\begin{equation*} \frac{\pi}{4}=\sum_{n=0}^\infty(-1)^n\frac{1}{2n+1} \text{.} \end{equation*}
